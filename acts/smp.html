SMP (Sounds of Mass Production)

Washington
Years Active: 1990s-2000s

Washington State Connection

SMP (Sounds of Mass Production) emerged from Washington State's industrial underground, embodying the region's complex relationship with industrial technology and mass production systems that shaped both the Pacific Northwest's economy and its artistic response to mechanization.

SMP (Sounds of Mass Production) represented the conceptual and intellectually rigorous side of Washington State's industrial music scene, creating compositions that served as both artistic statement and cultural critique of mass production systems. The project's name explicitly referenced the mechanized processes that defined much of the Pacific Northwest's industrial landscape, from aerospace manufacturing to software development.

Known for their sophisticated approach to industrial composition, SMP utilized precise drum programming, complex rhythmic structures, and carefully layered electronic elements to create music that reflected the efficiency and precision of industrial production while maintaining human creativity and emotional depth. Their work explored the intersection between human artistic expression and mechanical precision.

The project was part of Washington State's broader network of conceptually-driven electronic artists who were examining the implications of technological advancement and industrial culture through music. SMP's work represented the intellectual tradition within industrial music, creating compositions that functioned as both entertainment and philosophical inquiry into the nature of mass production and its effects on human creativity and community.
