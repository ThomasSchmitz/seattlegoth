Backandtotheleft

Seattle, Washington
[Band Photo - backandtotheleft.jpg]
Years Active: 2000s - 2010s
Washington State Connection: Seattle-based futurepop/synthpop trio formed from members of the local band Noxious Emotion, with strong ties to the Seattle electronic music scene.

Backandtotheleft (also stylized as BackAndToTheLeft) was a Seattle-based futurepop/synthpop trio whose members initially included Darren Miller, later featuring Brandon Jerwa on vocals, Megan "Fritter" Mace on drums, and Robert Wilhelm on keyboards. The band formed after their work in the local Seattle band Noxious Emotion, representing an evolution toward a more refined electronic sound.

The band's breakthrough came with their debut album "Obsolete," which was initially released on Tom Shear's 23db label in 2004. The connection to Assemblage 23's Tom Shear proved pivotal for the band's success, as Shear not only released their music but also produced their work. The album was later reissued by the prestigious Metropolis Records in 2005, bringing the band wider international attention.

Backandtotheleft's sound epitomized the futurepop movement of the mid-2000s, combining emotional vocals with driving electronic beats and synthesized melodies. Their music reflected both the optimism and melancholy that characterized the Seattle electronic scene during this period, making them an important part of the Pacific Northwest's contribution to the global futurepop movement.
