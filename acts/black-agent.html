Black Agent is a dark industrial/EBM project from Seattle, formed in 2016 by Lawrence "Larry" Kleinke as a solo endeavor that would evolve into one of the city's most compelling contemporary industrial acts. What began as Kleinke's personal exploration of harsh electronic sounds gradually expanded into a collaborative project that maintains the raw intensity of its origins while incorporating the dynamic energy of a full band.

By 2019, the project had evolved into a multi-member act with Neil Parker contributing guitar, bass, and live drums, bringing an organic rhythmic foundation to their electronic assault. Vocalist J Pit joined the lineup, adding a human voice to the mechanical chaos and helping to define Black Agent's signature sound. Their approach evokes the classic 1980s industrial and EBM traditions established by acts like Skinny Puppy and Front 242, while maintaining a distinctly modern and aggressive edge that reflects contemporary anxieties.

The band's discography demonstrates their commitment to exploring the darker aspects of human experience through electronic sound. Their debut album "Industrial Ruination" was released in April 2022, establishing their reputation for gritty sonic textures and uncompromising themes. This was followed by "Dehumanized" in 2023, which continued their exploration of societal decay, personal loss, and mental unrest. Both albums showcase their ability to combine the mechanical precision of classic EBM with the harsh, grinding sounds of modern industrial music.

Black Agent represents the ongoing evolution of Seattle's industrial music scene, serving as a bridge between the city's legendary 1990s industrial movement—which included acts like Skinny Puppy and Front Line Assembly—and the contemporary dark electronic underground. Their work demonstrates how new artists continue to find fresh ways to express the themes of alienation, technological anxiety, and social critique that have always been central to industrial music, ensuring that Seattle remains a vital center for dark electronic innovation.
