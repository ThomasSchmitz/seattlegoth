Washington State Connection

Named after Dante's deepest circle of hell, Ninth Circle emerged from Washington State's industrial underground, embodying the dark philosophical and literary traditions that influenced the Pacific Northwest's gothic scene.

Ninth Circle represented the heavier, more industrial side of Washington State's dark alternative scene, taking their name from Dante's Inferno and channeling literary darkness into crushing electronic compositions. The project emerged during the height of the 1990s industrial movement, when Washington State was producing some of the most innovative and intense electronic music in the underground scene.

Known for their uncompromising approach to industrial music, Ninth Circle created dense, layered compositions that incorporated elements of noise, power electronics, and rhythmic industrial. Their sound was characterized by heavy use of sampling, distorted vocals, and relentless drum programming that created an atmosphere of mechanical intensity and existential dread.

The project's influence extended through Washington State's underground network, where they were recognized for their technical sophistication and uncompromising artistic vision. Ninth Circle represented the intellectual and experimental aspects of the state's industrial scene, creating music that challenged listeners while maintaining the raw power that defined the genre during its most fertile period.
