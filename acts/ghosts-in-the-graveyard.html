Ghosts in the Graveyard

Seattle, Washington
Years Active: 2016-present

Washington State Connection

Ghosts in the Graveyard represents the collaborative spirit of Seattle's gothic and industrial music scene, featuring key members of other prominent Washington State acts. Led by Byron C. Miller, who also fronts Dracula Party and was formerly of God Module, the band exemplifies the interconnected nature of the Pacific Northwest's dark alternative music community.

Ghosts in the Graveyard is a Seattle-based act that blends deathrock, goth, darkwave, and alternative rock into theatrical, spooky soundscapes that pay homage to classic horror while maintaining a contemporary edge. Led by Byron C. Miller (also of Dracula Party and formerly of God Module), the band features a collaborative lineup including Paul Morgan, Kaylie Cortez, and Shelly Smith, creating a multi-dimensional approach to gothic performance that encompasses both musical and visual elements.

Their debut album "Monsters for the Masses" (2016) established the band's signature sound of dramatic, horror-themed compositions that draw equally from classic deathrock traditions and modern darkwave production techniques. The album features standout tracks like "Old Dark House," which showcases the band's ability to create atmospheric narratives through music while maintaining the driving rhythms essential to gothic dance culture. This release demonstrated their understanding of both the historical context of gothic music and its contemporary applications.

The theatrical nature of Ghosts in the Graveyard extends beyond their recorded material into their live performances, where the band incorporates visual elements and stage presentation that complement their horror-influenced musical themes. Their approach reflects Seattle's tradition of bands that understand performance as a total artistic experience, contributing to the city's reputation for innovative and engaging live music. The band maintains an active presence on Bandcamp, ensuring their full discography remains accessible to both longtime fans and newcomers to the gothic scene.
