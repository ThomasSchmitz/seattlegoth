Sky Cries Mary

Seattle, Washington
Years Active: Late 1980s-present

Washington State Connection

Sky Cries Mary is a seminal Seattle psychedelic/industrial/space-rock band formed in the late 1980s by Roderick Wolgamott, representing one of the most significant and long-running experimental acts to emerge from the Pacific Northwest. Their evolution from raw industrial noise to trance-like psychedelia embodies Seattle's musical adventurousness and the city's role as an incubator for genre-defying alternative music.

Sky Cries Mary formed in Seattle in the late 1980s under the creative direction of Roderick Wolgamott, beginning their musical journey with raw industrial noise before evolving into a distinctive psychedelic and space-rock sound. This transformation from harsh industrial beginnings to more atmospheric and psychedelic territories reflects the experimental spirit that has characterized much of Seattle's alternative music scene, where artists often push beyond single-genre constraints.

The band's discography spans over three decades, with key releases including "Until the Grinders Cease" (1989), "This Timeless Turning" (1994), and "Moonbathing on Sleeping Leaves" (1997), demonstrating their consistent artistic development and commitment to exploring different sonic territories. Their music incorporates trance-like psychedelia with space-rock elements, creating immersive compositions that have established them as pioneers in atmospheric alternative music.

Sky Cries Mary remains active with recent releases including "Everything Goes Somewhere" (2023) and "Immortal Jellyfish" (2025), showing their continued relevance and artistic vitality. Their comprehensive Bandcamp presence features albums such as "A Return to the Inner Experience," "Moonbathing on Sleeping Leaves," "Here & Now," and their complete catalog, making their extensive discography accessible to contemporary audiences and demonstrating their enduring influence on experimental and psychedelic music.
