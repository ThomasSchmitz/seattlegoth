Black Atmosphere

Seattle, Washington
[Band Photo - black-atmosphere.jpg]
Years Active: 1991 - 1999
Washington State Connection: Though formed in Los Angeles in 1991, Black Atmosphere relocated to Seattle in 1992 and became a legendary live act in the Pacific Northwest gothic scene throughout the 1990s.

Black Atmosphere was formed in 1991 in Los Angeles by Xstoph Gladis (also known as Christophe Gladis), a veteran of the California gothic scene who had previously performed guitar for prominent goth acts Christian Death, The Dream, Legion, and Black Cow. Initially conceived as a studio project, the band relocated to Seattle in 1992, where they transformed into one of the most celebrated and theatrical live acts in the Pacific Northwest's gothic and industrial scene, helping to establish Seattle as a major center for dark alternative music.

The Seattle incarnation of Black Atmosphere became legendary for their dramatic stage theatrics that went far beyond traditional rock performance, incorporating psychedelic and glam-gothic aesthetics with spectacular live shows that occasionally featured acrobats and fire-eaters. This commitment to theatrical presentation made them stand out even in Seattle's famously creative music scene. The band's lineup during their Seattle years evolved to include Stephen Garcia (drums) and Morgen Gallagher (bass), with later iterations featuring Tom Moller (drums) and Kenyatta Iniko (bass) during the recording of their first proper album.

Black Atmosphere's recorded output during their Seattle period included three major albums that documented their artistic evolution: "Redeem" (1995), which was their first proper album recorded between 1993 and 1995 and featured significant contributions from a keyboardist who joined in 1993; "Enrapture" (1997); and "Viral Transformations" (1999). The band also appeared on multiple compilations released by Cleopatra Records, gaining international exposure and sharing stages with many luminaries from the gothic and industrial scene throughout the 1990s.

Their music combined gothic rock with experimental and psychedelic elements, creating a unique sound that perfectly matched their theatrical live performances and distinguished them from both traditional gothic rock acts and the emerging industrial scene. The band's influence on Seattle's gothic community was profound and lasting, contributing to the city's reputation as a fertile ground for innovative dark alternative music. In 2020, their debut album "Redeem" was reissued as "Redeem (Isolation Masters 2020)" on Bandcamp, introducing their pioneering sound to a new generation of gothic music enthusiasts and preserving their legacy within the broader history of Pacific Northwest dark music.
