Black Atmosphere

Seattle, Washington
[Band Photo - black-atmosphere.jpg]
Years Active: 1991 - 1999
Washington State Connection: Though formed in Los Angeles in 1991, Black Atmosphere relocated to Seattle in 1992 and became a legendary live act in the Pacific Northwest gothic scene throughout the 1990s.

Black Atmosphere was formed in 1991 in Los Angeles by Xstoph Gladis, a former guitarist for prominent goth acts Christian Death, The Dream, Legion, and Black Cow. Initially a studio project, the band relocated to Seattle in 1992, where they transformed into one of the most celebrated live acts in the Pacific Northwest's gothic and industrial scene.

The Seattle incarnation of Black Atmosphere became renowned for their dramatic stage theatrics, psychedelic/glam-gothic aesthetics, and spectacular live performances that occasionally featured acrobats and fire-eaters. The band's lineup during their Seattle years included Christophe Gladis (vocals/guitar/keys), Stephen Garcia (drums), and Morgen Gallagher (bass), later featuring Tom Moller (drums) and Kenyatta Iniko (bass) on their first proper album.

Black Atmosphere released three major albums during their Seattle period: "Redeem" (1995), "Enrapture" (1997), and "Viral Transformations" (1999). They appeared on multiple compilations released by Cleopatra Records and shared stages with many luminaries from the gothic and industrial scene. Their music combined gothic rock with experimental and psychedelic elements, creating a unique sound that perfectly matched their theatrical live performances. The band's influence on Seattle's gothic scene was profound, helping to establish the city as a major center for dark alternative music in the 1990s.
