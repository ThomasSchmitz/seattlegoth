Washington State Connection

Dead Spells emerged from Seattle's vibrant darkwave and post-punk scene in the mid-2010s, contributing to the city's ongoing tradition of atmospheric and experimental music. The band was rooted in Seattle's underground music community, performing at local venues and becoming part of the network of artists that keep the Pacific Northwest's dark alternative music scene alive and evolving.

Dead Spells was a Seattle-based darkwave and post-punk band that created haunting, atmospheric music during their brief but impactful existence from 2015 to 2018. The band featured a talented lineup including Natalia Czajkiewicz, Christian Harding, Aaron Everett, David Audino, and Scott Snyder, who together crafted a sound that critics evocatively described as "funeral home rock" and "gothic Graceland." These unique characterizations captured the band's ability to blend mournful atmospheres with unexpected melodic elements and rhythmic sophistication.

The band's approach to darkwave music was both reverential to the genre's traditions and innovative in its execution. Their compositions featured layers of synthesizers, atmospheric guitar work, and haunting vocals that created immersive sonic landscapes designed to transport listeners into emotional territories both familiar and unsettling. The "funeral home rock" descriptor spoke to their ability to find beauty in melancholy and their skill at creating music that acknowledged loss while celebrating the complexity of human emotion.

The "gothic Graceland" comparison, noted by the Seattle Post-Intelligencer, suggested their remarkable ability to incorporate diverse musical influences into their dark aesthetic framework. This description highlighted how Dead Spells could take unexpected musical elements—perhaps rhythmic patterns or melodic structures reminiscent of different genres—and transform them through their gothic sensibility into something entirely new and compelling.

During their active years, Dead Spells released two notable EPs that documented their artistic evolution: their self-titled debut "Dead Spells" in 2015 established their signature atmospheric sound, while "What Remains" in 2018 served as both a culmination of their creative journey and a poignant farewell to their collaborative efforts. Both releases are available on Bandcamp, showcasing the band's commitment to exploring the darker corners of human emotion through carefully crafted electronic and post-punk compositions.

Though their time together was relatively short, Dead Spells left a lasting impression on Seattle's underground music scene and contributed meaningfully to the ongoing legacy of Pacific Northwest dark alternative music. Their ability to create evocative, emotionally resonant music within the constraints of a brief creative partnership demonstrates the continued vitality of Seattle's darkwave and post-punk communities.
