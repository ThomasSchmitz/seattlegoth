Washington State Connection

Dead Spells emerged from Seattle's vibrant darkwave and post-punk scene in the mid-2010s, contributing to the city's ongoing tradition of atmospheric and experimental music. The band was rooted in Seattle's underground music community, performing at local venues and becoming part of the network of artists that keep the Pacific Northwest's dark alternative music scene alive and evolving.

Dead Spells was a Seattle-based darkwave and post-punk band that created haunting, atmospheric music during their brief but impactful existence from 2015 to 2018. The band featured Natalia Czajkiewicz, Christian Harding, Aaron Everett, David Audino, and Scott Snyder, who together crafted a sound that critics evocatively described as "funeral home rock" and "gothic Graceland." This unique characterization captured the band's ability to blend mournful atmospheres with unexpected melodic elements and rhythmic sophistication.

The band's approach to darkwave music was both reverential to the genre's traditions and innovative in its execution. Their compositions featured layers of synthesizers, atmospheric guitar work, and haunting vocals that created immersive sonic landscapes. The "funeral home rock" descriptor spoke to their ability to find beauty in melancholy, while the "gothic Graceland" comparison suggested their skill in incorporating diverse musical influences into their dark aesthetic framework.

During their active years, Dead Spells released two notable EPs: their self-titled debut "Dead Spells" in 2015 and "What Remains" in 2018. These releases showcased the band's evolution and their commitment to exploring the darker corners of human emotion through music. Though their time together was relatively short, Dead Spells left a lasting impression on Seattle's underground music scene and contributed to the ongoing legacy of Pacific Northwest dark alternative music.
