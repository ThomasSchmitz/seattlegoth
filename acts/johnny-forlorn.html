Johnny Forlorn

Seattle, Washington
Years Active: 2000s - Present

Washington State Connection

Johnny Forlorn represents the continuation of Seattle's influential darkwave and gothic music legacy, having emerged from the city's interconnected scene that includes legendary acts like Faith & Disease. His work bridges different eras of Seattle's dark alternative music, connecting the pioneering ethereal wave movement of the 1990s with contemporary darkwave and new wave revival sounds.

Johnny Forlorn is a Seattle-based darkwave, new wave, and gothic artist who has become a significant figure in the Pacific Northwest's dark alternative music scene. His musical journey includes involvement with several notable Seattle acts, including Shadow Light, the pioneering ethereal wave band Faith & Disease, and Prayers for the Raven. This rich background in Seattle's gothic and darkwave underground has informed his solo work, which blends classic darkwave sensibilities with new wave energy and gothic atmosphere.

As a solo artist, Johnny Forlorn has developed a distinctive sound that draws from multiple decades of dark alternative music while maintaining a contemporary edge. His approach combines the atmospheric elements characteristic of Seattle's ethereal wave movement with the driving rhythms and accessibility of new wave, creating music that appeals to both longtime fans of the gothic scene and newcomers to the genre. His experience with Faith & Disease, one of Seattle's most respected ethereal wave acts, particularly informs his understanding of atmospheric composition and emotional depth.

Johnny Forlorn maintains an active presence through both his official website at johnnyforlorn.com and a robust Bandcamp catalog that includes releases such as "A Fifth of Johnny," "Calling the Quarters," and "It Hurts When I Laugh." These releases showcase his range as a songwriter and his ability to navigate different moods within the darkwave and gothic spectrum. His continued activity in Seattle's music scene demonstrates the ongoing vitality of the city's dark alternative community, carrying forward the legacy established by earlier pioneering acts while contributing his own creative voice to the evolution of Pacific Northwest gothic and darkwave music.
