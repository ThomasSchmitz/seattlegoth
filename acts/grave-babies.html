Washington State Connection

Grave Babies emerged from Seattle's vibrant underground music scene in 2009, becoming a significant fixture in the Pacific Northwest's gothic rock and darkwave community. The band has consistently operated from Seattle, contributing to the city's reputation as a breeding ground for dark alternative music alongside acts like Dead Spells and Faith & Disease.

Grave Babies formed in Seattle in 2009, quickly establishing themselves as a formidable force in the gothic rock, darkwave, and garage/noise rock scene. The band's sound combines the raw energy of garage rock with the atmospheric darkness of gothic and darkwave music, creating a unique sonic landscape that has resonated with fans of dark alternative music throughout their career.

The band's discography spans over a decade of evolution, beginning with their debut album "Deathface" in 2009, which introduced their distinctive blend of gothic sensibilities and garage rock aggression. This was followed by "Crusher" in 2013, which saw the band refining their sound and gaining wider recognition within the darkwave community. Their 2015 release "Holographic Violence" marked a significant creative milestone, showcasing the band's ability to balance melodic elements with their characteristically dark aesthetic.

After several years of development, Grave Babies returned in 2023 with "Death Will Kill Us All," demonstrating their continued relevance and artistic growth within the gothic rock scene. The band maintains an active presence on Bandcamp, where fans can access their complete catalog including tracks from the "Gothdammit" EP and their full-length albums. Their live performances in the Seattle area have become legendary among local goth and darkwave enthusiasts, cementing their status as one of the city's premier dark alternative acts.
