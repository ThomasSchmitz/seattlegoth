Washington State Connection

Kill Switch…Klick was founded in Seattle in 1991 by composer and singer d.A. Sebasstian, establishing itself as one of the pioneering industrial-rock crossover acts in the Pacific Northwest underground scene. The band has maintained its Seattle base throughout its career, contributing to the city's diverse dark electronic music landscape.

Kill Switch…Klick emerged from Seattle's burgeoning alternative music scene in 1991 as an industrial crossover project that would help define the city's dark electronic underground. Founded by composer and vocalist d.A. Sebasstian, the project initially began as a solo endeavor exploring the intersection of industrial music with rock sensibilities, creating a sound that bridged the gap between the harsh electronics of pure industrial and the accessibility of alternative rock.

The band's evolution took a significant turn when drummer Mike Ditmore joined the lineup, bringing a dynamic rhythmic foundation that enhanced the project's live performance capabilities. This collaboration allowed Kill Switch…Klick to develop a more complex and layered sound, incorporating live drumming with programmed elements to create their signature industrial-rock blend. The duo became known locally for their innovative approach to combining electronic manipulation with traditional rock instrumentation.

Throughout the 1990s and into the new millennium, Kill Switch…Klick maintained an active presence in Seattle's underground music scene, developing a reputation for their compelling live performances and their role in pioneering the industrial-rock crossover sound that would influence many subsequent Pacific Northwest acts. Their Bandcamp presence demonstrates their continued activity and commitment to the Seattle scene, while their early work remains influential among fans of industrial crossover music in the region.
