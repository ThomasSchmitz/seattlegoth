The Bad Things is a Seattle-based "junkyard cabaret" ensemble that formed in 2002, creating a theatrical and chaotic punk-folk style that defies easy categorization within traditional music genres. Their approach combines elements of cabaret performance with punk energy and folk instrumentation, resulting in a distinctive sound that occupies a unique space within Seattle's diverse alternative music community. While described as "dark-adjacent" rather than strictly gothic, their theatrical presentation connects them to the broader network of Seattle's experimental performance artists.

The ensemble's "junkyard cabaret" aesthetic emphasizes theatrical chaos and unconventional instrumentation, creating performances that blend musical composition with performance art elements. This approach reflects Seattle's tradition of supporting experimental and boundary-pushing artists who challenge conventional presentation formats. Their work demonstrates how the city's alternative music scene encompasses not just specific genres but also innovative approaches to combining music with other performance elements.
