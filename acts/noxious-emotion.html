Washington State Connection

Noxious Emotion was a Seattle electronic dance-industrial act that formed in 1992, emerging from the Northwest's industrial underground during the height of the grunge era. While Seattle was gaining international recognition for its rock music, Noxious Emotion represented the city's parallel development of electronic and industrial music, contributing to a diverse underground scene that extended beyond the mainstream guitar-driven sound for which the city became famous.

The band made a distinct impact within US industrial and gothic club scenes throughout the 1990s and early 2000s, developing a sound that combined danceable electronic rhythms with industrial production techniques. Their approach to electronic music emphasized club-friendly compositions that could energize dance floors while maintaining the darker aesthetic sensibilities characteristic of industrial music. This balance between accessibility and underground appeal helped establish them within the broader national industrial music network.

Their Bandcamp presence features archival releases including "Senses" (2003), which captures their electronic dance-industrial approach during their most active period. The band's influence extended beyond their own releases through member involvement in subsequent Seattle electronic projects, most notably their connection to Backandtotheleft, whose members initially emerged from Noxious Emotion before developing into one of Seattle's significant futurepop acts on Tom Shear's 23db label.
