Noise Box

Washington
Years Active: 1990s-2000s

Washington State Connection

Noise Box emerged from Washington State's experimental electronic underground, contributing to the Pacific Northwest's reputation for boundary-pushing industrial and noise music during the height of the alternative music explosion.

Noise Box represented the experimental and abrasive edge of Washington State's industrial music scene, creating harsh electronic compositions that challenged conventional notions of rhythm, melody, and song structure. The project emerged during the 1990s when the Pacific Northwest was becoming known not just for grunge, but for diverse underground electronic music that pushed boundaries.

Known for their uncompromising approach to sound design and composition, Noise Box utilized a wide range of electronic equipment, sampling techniques, and processing methods to create dense walls of organized chaos. Their music occupied the intersection between industrial, noise, and power electronics, creating immersive soundscapes that were both challenging and rewarding for dedicated listeners.

The project was part of a network of Washington State experimental electronic artists who were exploring the possibilities of digital and analog synthesis, contributing to a regional sound that would influence underground electronic music throughout the Pacific Northwest. Noise Box's work represented the avant-garde spirit that helped establish Washington State as a center for innovative dark electronic music.
